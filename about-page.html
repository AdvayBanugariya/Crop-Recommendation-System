About the Crop Recommendation System

Understanding Agricultural Parameters

Our crop recommendation system uses seven crucial parameters to determine the most suitable crop for your field. Each parameter plays a vital role in plant growth and development. Below, you'll find detailed information about each parameter and its importance in agriculture.

Nitrogen (N)

Key component for:

  • Leaf and stem growth
  • Chlorophyll production
  • Protein synthesis
  • Crop yield and quality

Optimal range: 0-140 kg/ha

Phosphorus (P)

Essential for:

  • Root development
  • Energy transfer
  • Flowering and fruiting
  • Seed formation

Optimal range: 5-145 kg/ha

Potassium (K)

Critical for:

  • Disease resistance
  • Water regulation
  • Fruit quality
  • Overall plant health

Optimal range: 5-205 kg/ha

Temperature

Influences:

  • Seed germination
  • Growth rate
  • Photosynthesis
  • Crop development stages

Optimal range: 7-45Â°C

Humidity

Affects:

  • Water uptake
  • Transpiration rate
  • Disease susceptibility
  • Plant stress levels

Optimal range: 10-100%

pH Level

Important for:

  • Nutrient availability
  • Soil microorganism activity
  • Root growth
  • Nutrient absorption

Optimal range: 3-10 pH

Rainfall

Critical for:

  • Water availability
  • Soil moisture content
  • Nutrient dissolution
  • Overall crop growth

Optimal range: 20-500 mm
